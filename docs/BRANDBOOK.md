# AgentsOX Brandbook

Version: 1.1  
Owner: AgentsOX  
Brand promise: custom AI systems for business operations, built until the client trusts the workflow.

AgentsOX is a founder-led technical agency for small businesses, clinics, real-estate offices, coaches, e-commerce stores, and local service operators that need custom AI systems but do not want a generic product forced onto their workflow. The brand should feel technical and premium, warm enough to earn trust, and quiet enough for serious non-technical buyers. It should never feel like AI hype, a corporate consultancy, or a developer-only shop.

## 0. Brand Core and Positioning

### Positioning Statement

For small business owners and non-technical operators dealing with legacy systems, missed leads, manual admin, and messy workflows, AgentsOX is the custom AI automation partner that listens first, ships fast, and keeps improving the system until the client feels safe using it, because every engagement is built around the client's real process instead of a pre-made product.

### 10-Second Belief

Buyers should believe this after 10 seconds:

> AgentsOX can understand my business problem and build a practical system that solves it.

### Case-Study Belief

Buyers should believe this after reading a case study:

> AgentsOX executes, integrates, and supports real business workflows, not just AI demos.

### Mission

Help small and mid-sized businesses use AI to improve daily operations without losing control, trust, or clarity.

### Vision

Become the practical AI systems partner for businesses that want modern automation but need a human guide who can listen, explain, build, and stay accountable.

### Values

- Listen before building: understand the client's real workflow, constraints, team habits, and fears before proposing a system.
- Custom over generic: do not force clients into a fixed product when their business needs a tailored workflow.
- Ship fast, then harden: deliver useful working systems quickly, then improve reliability, monitoring, and edge-case handling.
- Safety creates adoption: the job is not done until the client understands the system and feels safe trusting it.
- Business outcome first: AI is only valuable when it saves time, captures revenue, reduces manual work, or gives better visibility.

### First Buyer Wedge

Primary early buyers:

- Clinics, MedSpas, and aesthetics clinics.
- Local service businesses and session-based operators.
- Real-estate offices.
- Coaches and appointment-based experts.
- E-commerce stores.

Secondary buyers:

- Freelancers with repeatable client operations.
- Small teams using legacy systems.
- Non-technical business owners who know the pain but do not know how to specify the technical solution.

### What AgentsOX Is Not

- Not a generic chatbot vendor.
- Not a SaaS tool trying to fit every client into one product.
- Not an AI hype agency selling "magic".
- Not a corporate consultancy that leaves clients with a slide deck.
- Not a developer-only shop that expects the client to translate business pain into technical specs.

## 0.1 Offer Architecture

The first commercial offer is `Automation Build`: a custom AI or workflow automation designed around a client's current business process.

### Core Offers

1. AI Chatbots
   - Website, WhatsApp, or internal chat assistants for lead capture, FAQs, routing, booking, and business-specific workflows.
   - Must include escalation rules and human handoff.

2. Business Automations
   - Scheduling, reminders, lead routing, CRM updates, follow-ups, document handling, notifications, and repetitive admin workflows.
   - Must include clear failure behavior and ownership.

3. Business Analytics
   - Dashboards and reports that help owners see leads, bookings, revenue signals, operations, and bottlenecks.
   - Must focus on decisions, not decorative charts.

4. Business Optimization
   - Workflow review and implementation support to remove manual steps, reduce missed opportunities, and make existing systems work together.
   - May include AI, no-code tools, scripts, or custom software.

5. Workshops and Enablement
   - Practical training for teams that need to understand how to use AI tools safely in daily operations.
   - Must be hands-on and connected to the client's real business.

### Offer Ladder

- Discovery call: understand pain, urgency, current tools, and trust concerns.
- Workflow audit: map the current process, failure points, data flow, and automation opportunities.
- Automation build: ship a working first version.
- Trust hardening: add logging, review points, handoff, monitoring, and documentation.
- Retainer or support: maintain, optimize, and expand the system.

### Pricing Posture

The brand should support a wide range of pricing, from small workshops and focused pilots to larger custom builds and retainers. Do not make the brand feel cheap. Even when the first project is small, the visual and verbal identity should signal serious implementation quality.

## 0.2 Trust Architecture

AgentsOX wins by making non-technical clients feel understood and safe.

### Trust Promise

> We listen, build around your real workflow, explain the system clearly, and keep improving it until you trust it.

### Required Trust Signals

Every high-intent page, proposal, or case study should answer:

- What problem did the client have?
- What existing tools or legacy systems were involved?
- What did AgentsOX build?
- What changed in the workflow?
- Where does a human approve or review?
- What happens when the automation fails?
- What data is touched and where does it go?
- How is the system monitored or maintained?
- What proof shows it worked?

### Objection Handling

AI is unreliable:

- Say: "We design workflows with human checkpoints, fallback paths, and clear failure behavior."
- Do not say: "The AI handles everything automatically."

Data privacy:

- Say: "We minimize the data used, document where it flows, and avoid unnecessary retention."
- Do not say: "Trust us, your data is safe."

Too expensive:

- Say: "We start from the business problem and size the build to the value of the workflow."
- Do not lead with technology cost.

AI will replace humans:

- Say: "The system removes repetitive work and keeps people in control of sensitive decisions."
- Avoid language that celebrates replacing staff.

Hard to maintain:

- Say: "We document the system, define ownership, and offer support paths after launch."
- Include recovery behavior in every serious automation.

Vendor lock-in:

- Say: "We prefer clear handoff, documented systems, and tools the client can understand."
- Avoid unnecessary proprietary dependency when a simpler maintainable option works.

## 0.3 Proof and Case Studies

Proof should be specific, visual, and operational. Avoid broad claims.

### Approved Proof Types

- Client work.
- Screenshots.
- Demos.
- Automations built.
- Before/after workflow maps.
- Technical case studies.
- Short client quotes when available.

### Case Study Format

```text
Client context:
MedSpa / local service business / real-estate office / e-commerce store.

Problem:
What was slow, manual, missed, confusing, or risky?

Current tools:
WhatsApp, website form, CRM, Google Calendar, email, spreadsheet, booking tool, legacy system.

Build:
What AgentsOX built in plain business language.

Human control:
Where a person reviews, approves, or takes over.

Failure behavior:
What happens if an API, model, or workflow step fails.

Result:
Time saved, leads captured, faster response, better visibility, fewer manual steps, or improved owner confidence.
```

### Screenshot Rules

- Show real workflow states when possible: lead received, task created, dashboard updated, approval pending, failed step recovered.
- Blur private client data.
- Do not use fake dashboards as proof.
- Add short captions explaining the business outcome.

## 0.4 Channel Rules

### Website

The website must quickly answer:

- Who this is for.
- What problems AgentsOX solves.
- What the first engagement looks like.
- Why the client can trust the system.
- How to start a conversation.

Primary website CTA:

- "Book a workflow audit"
- "Show me what you want to automate"
- "Start with one business problem"

Avoid CTAs that feel vague:

- "Transform your business"
- "Unlock AI"
- "Get started with the future"

### Personalized Email

Email should feel researched and specific. Lead with a real business observation, not AI excitement.

Structure:

```text
I noticed [specific business workflow / lead source / booking path].
There may be an opportunity to reduce [manual work / missed lead risk / follow-up delay].
AgentsOX builds custom automations around existing workflows, not off-the-shelf products.
Worth a short conversation about one workflow you want to improve?
```

### WhatsApp

Use WhatsApp for warm, direct, low-friction communication. Keep messages short and clear. Never send long technical explanations unless the client asks.

### Workshop Landing Page

Workshop pages must promise practical capability, not inspiration. Use language like:

- "Bring one workflow. Leave with a working AI-assisted process."
- "Learn how to use AI safely in your daily operations."
- "Build something your team can actually reuse."

## 0.5 Naming and Brand Architecture

AgentsOX is the company brand. Do not center Nadav or `nadavox` in the public brand system. Founder-led trust can appear through direct language, high accountability, and personal sales conversations, but the brand should be able to grow beyond one person.

The ox symbol represents business resilience, strength, and dependable execution. It should not be explained as a personal name reference in public brand copy.

## 1. Logo Specifications and Systems

### Primary Logo

Use the horizontal lockup when there is enough space:

- Mark: geometric ox head with circuit paths and a central precision arrow.
- Wordmark: `AgentsOX`.
- Optional descriptor: `AI systems for business operations`.
- Preferred background: `#0A0A0F` or `#12121A`.
- Preferred asset: `/brand/agentsox-logo-lockup.svg`.

The ox mark connects business resilience, operational strength, and technical execution. It should not be framed publicly as a personal founder reference.

### Secondary / Stacked Logo

Use the mark above the wordmark for square placements, presentation covers, pitch decks, thumbnails, and vertical layouts.

Rules:

- Center-align the mark and wordmark.
- Keep the mark at roughly `1.2x` the wordmark height.
- Do not include the descriptor below `480px` wide.

### Icon-Only

Use the standalone mark for favicons, avatars, app icons, social profile photos, and compact UI.

- Preferred asset: `/brand/agentsox-mark.svg`.
- Minimum favicon version: simplify to the outer ox silhouette, central arrow, and no more than three node circles.
- At `16px`, remove internal circuit lines if they blur.
- At `32px+`, circuit lines may remain if they are still crisp.

### Clear Space

Define `X` as the height of the `O` in `OX`.

- Primary lockup clear space: at least `0.5X` on every side.
- Icon-only clear space: at least `0.25` of the icon width on every side.
- Do not place navigation labels, badges, borders, or partner logos inside the clear-space zone.

### Minimum Sizes

Digital:

- Primary lockup with descriptor: `360px` wide minimum.
- Primary lockup without descriptor: `220px` wide minimum.
- Stacked logo: `160px` wide minimum.
- Icon-only: `24px` minimum for UI, `16px` only for favicon.

Print:

- Primary lockup with descriptor: `55mm` wide minimum.
- Primary lockup without descriptor: `35mm` wide minimum.
- Icon-only: `8mm` wide minimum.

### Incorrect Usage

Do not:

- Stretch or compress the mark.
- Rotate the mark or wordmark.
- Change the gradient direction.
- Add heavy glow, shadows, bevels, chrome, or 3D effects.
- Place the logo on bright saturated backgrounds that fight the cyan/blue accents.
- Put the logo over busy photography without a dark overlay.
- Use the ox head as an aggressive mascot or full-body animal.
- Rebuild the wordmark in a random typeface.
- Add extra taglines below the lockup without approval.

Primary accent gradient:


- Use color plus text/icon labels for status states.

## 3. Typography and Hierarchy

### Typefaces

Primary structural typeface:

- `Plus Jakarta Sans`
- Use for headings, wordmark-aligned display, navigation, buttons, and section labels.

Secondary/body typeface:

- `Figtree`
- Use for body copy, descriptions, cards, forms, documentation, and marketing text.

Technical typeface:

- `JetBrains Mono`
- Use for code, raw system values, tokens, logs, technical labels, and compact metadata.

### Allowed Weights

- Body regular: `400`
- Body medium: `500`
- UI semibold: `600`
- Heading bold: `700`
- Hero/display extra bold: `800`

Do not introduce additional font weights unless a specific asset requires it.

### Type Scale

Use a Minor Third-inspired scale with pragmatic web sizes.

## 4. Visual Assets and UI/UX Style

### Iconography

- Use outline icons with rounded caps and joins.
- Default stroke width: `2px` at `24px`, `2.25px` to `2.5px` for emphasized actions.
- Icons should feel geometric and operational, matching the logo linework.
- Avoid filled emoji-like icons, novelty illustrations, and overly detailed AI brain imagery.

### Imagery Direction

Use:

- Real screenshots of shipped systems.
- Clean dashboard crops with visible data density.
- Dark UI surfaces with cyan/blue accent focus.
- Precise implementation details: automations, calendar flows, communication routing, approval states, analytics, monitoring, and recovery paths.
- Business-context imagery only when it clarifies the client workflow.

Avoid:

- Generic glowing robots.
- Abstract AI faces.
- Purple neon tunnels.
- Busy stock photos of business teams pointing at screens.
- Fake dashboards with unreadable filler data.
- Founder lifestyle imagery unless it directly supports trust in delivery.

### UI Components

Radii:

- Buttons: `8px` to `12px`.
- Cards and panels: `8px`.
- Modals and large containers: `12px`.
- Pills and tags: `999px` only when the element is truly a pill.

Spacing:

- Base unit: `4px`.
- Common scale: `4, 8, 12, 16, 24, 32, 48, 64, 96`.
- Section max width: `1126px`.
- Body copy max width: `65ch`.

Buttons:

- Primary: solid `ox-blue` or cyan-to-blue gradient.
- Secondary: transparent/dark surface with `ox-border`.
- Focus: visible `2px` outline using `ox-cyan` or `ox-blue`.
- Do not use decorative glows except on hover/focus, and keep them subtle.

Cards:

- Prefer quiet surfaces and thin borders.
- Do not nest cards inside cards.
- Use content density appropriate for operational software, not a decorative landing page.

## 5. Voice and Verbal Identity

### Tone Pillars

Direct:

- Say what the system does.
- Prefer concrete verbs: build, route, monitor, recover, schedule, ship.
- Avoid vague AI claims like "unlock potential" or "revolutionize workflows".

Engineer-focused:

- Mention tools and constraints when relevant.
- Explain reliability, control, and implementation details.
- Use proof and examples over hype.

Calm authority:

- Confident, practical, and concise.
- No overpromising autonomy.
- Human control remains explicit for sensitive workflows.

Warm accountability:

- Sound like a builder who will stay with the client until the workflow is understood and trusted.
- Use plain explanations for non-technical buyers.
- Avoid hiding behind technical language when the client needs business clarity.

### Messaging Rules

Use:

- "Custom AI systems for business operations"
- "Automation that actually works"
- "Human in the loop"
- "Built to scale"
- "Built around your real workflow"
- "One business problem at a time"
- "We stay until the system feels safe"
- "Practical automation for clinics and local businesses"

Avoid:

- "Magic"
- "10x everything"
- "Fully autonomous with no oversight"
- "AI revolution"
- "Disrupt your industry"
- "Plug-and-play for every business"
- "AI agent workforce"
- "Replace your team"
- "Universal AI platform"

### Formatting Mechanics

- Write `AgentsOX` exactly with capital `A`, `OX`.
- Do not lead public brand copy with `Nadav Oxenberg` or `nadavox`; AgentsOX is the company brand.
- Founder-led accountability can appear in sales calls, proposals, and about-page context when it increases trust.
- Write `AI`, `MCP`, `API`, `CRM`, and `SLA` in uppercase.
- Do not lead with tool names in marketing copy unless the buyer already cares about the tool.
- Use short sentences in UI.
- Error messages should state what happened, why if known, and what to do next.
