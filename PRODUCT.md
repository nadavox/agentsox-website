# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Small-business owners and non-technical operators: clinics, MedSpas and aesthetics
clinics, real-estate offices, coaches and appointment-based experts, local service
operators, and e-commerce stores. They feel the pain of missed leads, manual admin,
legacy systems, and messy workflows, but cannot specify the technical solution. They
arrive skeptical of AI hype and need to believe, within ten seconds, that AgentsOX
can understand their business problem and build a practical system that solves it.
Their job to be done: decide whether to book a workflow audit.

## Product Purpose

The public website for AgentsOX, a founder-led custom AI and automation agency. It
presents the brand, services, proof, and workshop offer, and runs a client-facing
intake bot that answers FAQs, shapes a workflow brief, and submits it by email.
Success is the visitor booking a workflow audit; the intake bot is the secondary
path that warms and qualifies that conversion. The site must read as technical and
premium, warm enough to earn trust, quiet enough for serious non-technical buyers,
and never like AI hype, a corporate consultancy, or a developer-only shop.

## Brand Personality

Direct, calm, concrete. Speak to "you"; the company is "we"; the brand is always
`AgentsOX`. Lead with specific verbs (build, route, monitor, recover, schedule) and
real business outcomes, not adjectives. The promise: custom AI systems for business
operations, built until the client trusts the workflow. Listen first, ship fast,
then harden. No emoji, no em dashes (hyphens only), no hype vocabulary. Concrete CTAs
("Book a workflow audit"), never "Unlock AI" or "Transform your business". Internal
framings ("CTO-as-a-service", "solution factory") never appear in public copy.

## Anti-references

- AI hype: glowing robots, abstract AI faces, purple/neon gradient tunnels, "magic",
  "10x", "revolutionize", "disrupt", "AI workforce".
- Generic SaaS marketing: cream/sand body backgrounds, tiny tracked uppercase eyebrows
  above every section, numbered section markers as default scaffolding, the hero-metric
  template, identical icon-heading-text card grids.
- Corporate consultancy stiffness and developer-only shop coldness.
- Fake dashboards with filler data presented as proof; unblurred private client data.

## Design Principles

- **Listen first, then prove.** Structure favors understanding the buyer's problem and
  showing real, executed workflows over selling features.
- **Quiet confidence over hype.** Restraint is the brand's credibility signal; dark,
  technical, premium, never loud.
- **Concrete over abstract.** Specific nouns, verbs, and real outcomes everywhere, in
  copy and in imagery. Show the system, not a metaphor for it.
- **Trust is the conversion.** Every surface lowers the perceived risk of booking a call
  for a non-technical, skeptical buyer.
- **One source of truth.** Every brand value reads from `tokens.json`; nothing is
  hardcoded, so the site stays coherent as it grows.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA as the floor. The brand token system is engineered to meet it
(text hits AAA on the dark base; action fills and borders are tuned for AA). The audit
holds every surface to AA: contrast, visible focus, keyboard operability, semantic
structure, labeled forms, and a reduced-motion path for every animation.
